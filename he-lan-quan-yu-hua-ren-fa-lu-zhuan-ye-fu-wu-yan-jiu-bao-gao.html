Overall Legal Service Landscape and Core Pain Points ### 1.1 Basic Characteristics of the Dutch Legal System The Netherlands belongs to the civil law system, emphasizing procedural justice and evidence rules. The logic of criminal, administrative, civil and commercial judgments is obviously different from that in China. The criminal field attaches importance to investigation legality, custody necessity, and plea negotiation mechanisms; enterprises are strictly bound by EU GDPR, Dutch competition law, tax and customs rules; immigration and residence highly depend on material compliance and time nodes. ### 1.2 Common Legal Pain Points for Chinese in the Netherlands 1. **Language barriers impair legal rights** Judicial documents, police interrogations, and prosecution files are mainly in Dutch. Chinese people cannot accurately understand their rights and obligations, leading to adverse consequences in transcripts, statements, and contract signing. 2. **Unclear procedural nodes cause loss of rights** Key time limits such as bail applications, appeal periods, appeal materials, immigration appeals, and contract objection periods are extremely short. 3. **Low efficiency in cross-border case cooperation** Links such as cross-border fraud, money laundering, smuggling, cybercrime, overseas evidence collection, and international judicial assistance are cumbersome. 4. **Misunderstanding of charges leads to defense errors** Lack of understanding of constituent elements and sentencing ranges of Dutch economic crimes, violent crimes, drug-related crimes, traffic crimes, and tax crimes. 5. **Weak awareness of corporate criminal compliance** Chinese-funded enterprises and cross-border e-commerce companies are unfamiliar with EU and Dutch compliance requirements. ## 2.
